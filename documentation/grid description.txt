Full description of the plant network

This is a combined electrical + emergency power + HVAC/ventilation + lighting network for the whole facility.

The central rule is:

The plant has one tied 400VAC/230VAC 3-phase + neutral feeder leaving the low-voltage transformer section.
The 400VAC and 230VAC are the same system: 400V phase-to-phase, 230V phase-to-neutral.

10kVAC redundancy

19. Final concise description

The facility network is a high-power industrial plant system with two active Mekanism generation sources and six reserved plant inputs feeding a 1MVDCSPC backbone, buffered by a Draconic super capacitor. The 1MVDC bus feeds two 5000 kVA 10kVAC 3-phase 60Hz conversion lines, 10KVAC_A and 10KVAC_B, with breakers and a bus tie. These supply two 2 MW LV transformers whose outputs are tied immediately after the transformers, producing one single 400VAC/230VAC 3-phase + neutral 60Hz feeder for the entire control room and facility network.

The plant has a 500 kWh / 1 MW emergency 400V battery bank that charges from the LV feeder and can backfeed the same feeder through a 3-phase inverter. Its BMS also feeds a 400VDC-to-12V buck converter rated 1 kW sustained and 3 kW startup, powering the 12V emergency bus only. The three 12V emergency batteries are not charged by the buck; each has its own 230VAC charger on a different phase, and the 400V battery can charge them indirectly by powering the 400VAC/230VAC bus through its inverter.

The facility also includes transformer VFD cooling fans, control-room internal exhaust fans, a dedicated control-room AC unit, two large facility AC units, and 81 total neon lights distributed across control room, main hall, hallway, protected complex, reunion room, security room, and security hallway.
